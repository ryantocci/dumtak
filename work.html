Our work

Four ways we bring rhythm to children.

Dum Tak isn't a performance series. It's a community-based framework — designed to integrate live music, listening, and creativity into the everyday environments where children already live and learn.

♫

Music education

Weekly rhythm-based sessions in preschools and early-learning centers. Each session is built around a developmental milestone — listening, turn-taking, motor coordination — and led by a working musician trained in early-childhood pedagogy.

ForAges 1–6
Format30–45 min weekly
Group10–15 children
◉

Impromptu performance

Pop-up live music in libraries, parks, farmers' markets, and other community gathering spaces — free, drop-in, and designed for families to participate alongside children. The point isn't to watch; it's to join.

ForAll ages
FormatPublic, free
FrequencyWeekly · year-round
⚉

Immersive orchestra

A touch-and-play experience where children meet real musicians and explore real instruments. Strings, percussion, woodwinds — all at child height, all available to handle. We bring the orchestra down off the stage.

ForAges 3–10
FormatQuarterly events
Hosted bySchools, libraries
♥

Music for healing

Bedside and clinical visits in pediatric and family healthcare settings, in partnership with hospital child-life and music-therapy teams. Rhythm and song reduce procedural anxiety, support regulation, and remind children that they are still children.

ForPediatric patients & families
FormatBy referral
PartnersMA hospitals
