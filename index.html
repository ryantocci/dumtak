Why "Dum Tak"
My son couldn't yet say "drum," but he could say Dum… Tak — the two beats his father played for him every morning. That was his first word for music, and the first thing he taught me about how children learn.
— Founder's note
Your role

You are the instrument.

You don't need to be a musician. A clap, a tap, a hummed lullaby — these are the tools that shape a child's mind.

01

Tap the everyday

Turn a stroller walk, a stirring spoon, or bath time into a shared beat.

02

Sing imperfectly

Your voice is the most important instrument your child will ever hear.

03

Move together

Sway, rock, dance — co-regulation begins in synchronized motion.

How we show up

Four ways we bring rhythm to your community.

Hands-on music for early learning centers, schools, healthcare settings, and public spaces across Massachusetts.

♫

Music education

Weekly rhythm sessions in preschools and early-learning centers, designed around the developmental milestones of the 0–7 window.

Ongoing program
◉

Impromptu performance

Pop-up live music in libraries, parks, farmers' markets, and community gathering spaces — free and open to families.

Year-round
⚉

Immersive orchestra

Touch-and-play sessions where children meet real musicians and explore real instruments with their hands.

Quarterly events
♥

Music for healing

Bedside and clinical visits in pediatric and family healthcare settings, in partnership with hospital child-life teams.
